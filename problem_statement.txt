<p><strong>ATOMQUEST HACKATHON 1.0</strong></p><p>Problem Statement</p><p><strong>In-House Goal Setting &amp; Tracking Portal</strong></p><h1>1.  Background &amp; Problem Context</h1><p>Organizations that rely on manual or fragmented goal-tracking methods often struggle with alignment, visibility, and accountability. Spreadsheets, emails, and offline review cycles create blind spots — managers cannot monitor team progress in real time, employees lack clarity on how their work connects to organizational priorities, and HR teams are left piecing together data at appraisal time.</p><p>The challenge is to build a structured, digital Goal Setting &amp; Tracking Portal that eliminates these pain points. The system must support the full lifecycle of employee goals — from creation and alignment to quarterly check-ins and performance visibility — while being intuitive, reliable, and audit-ready.</p><h1>2.  What You Need to Build</h1><p>Participants are required to design and develop a functional web-based portal that fulfils the following core requirements.</p><h2>2.1  Phase 1 — Goal Creation &amp; Approval (Must-Have)</h2><ul><li>Employee-facing interface to create and submit a Goal Sheet</li><li>Select a Thrust Area and define Goal Title / Description</li><li>Assign Unit of Measurement (UoM): Numeric, %, Timeline, or Zero-based</li><li>Set Targets and Weightage per goal</li><li>System-enforced validation rules:</li><li>Total weightage across all goals must equal 100%</li><li>Minimum weightage per individual goal: 10%</li><li>Maximum number of goals per employee: 8</li><li>Manager (L1) Approval Workflow:</li><li>Review submitted goals; ability to edit targets / weightages inline or return for rework</li><li>On approval, goals are locked — no further edits without Admin intervention</li><li>Shared Goals functionality:</li><li>Admin or manager can push a departmental KPI to multiple employees</li><li>Recipients may adjust weightage only; Goal Title and Target are read-only</li><li>Achievement updates by the primary owner sync across all linked goal sheets</li></ul><h2>2.2  Phase 2 — Achievement Tracking &amp; Quarterly Check-ins (Must-Have)</h2><ul><li>Quarterly update interface for employees to log Actual Achievement against Planned Targets</li><li>Status selection per goal: Not Started / On Track / Completed</li><li>Manager Check-in module:</li><li>View Planned vs. Achievement data for each team member</li><li>Add a structured Check-in Comment to document the discussion</li><li>System-computed progress scores (for tracking only, not ratings):</li></ul><table><tr><td><p><strong>UoM Type</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Description</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Formula</strong></p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Min (Numeric / %)</p></td><td><p>Higher is better — e.g., Sales Revenue</p></td><td><p>Achievement ÷ Target</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Max (Numeric / %)</p></td><td><p>Lower is better — e.g., TAT, Cost</p></td><td><p>Target ÷ Achievement</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Timeline</p></td><td><p>Date-based completion</p></td><td><p>Completion date vs. Deadline</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Zero</p></td><td><p>Zero = Success — e.g., Safety incidents</p></td><td><p>If 0 → 100%, else 0%</p></td></tr></table><h2>2.3  Check-in Schedule</h2><p>The portal must enforce the following quarterly windows for achievement capture:</p><table><tr><td><p><strong>Period</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Window Opens</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Action</strong></p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Phase 1 — Goal Setting</strong></p></td><td><p>1st May</p></td><td><p>Goal Creation, Submission &amp; Approval</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Q1 Check-in</strong></p></td><td><p>July</p></td><td><p>Progress Update — Planned vs. Actual</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Q2 Check-in</strong></p></td><td><p>October</p></td><td><p>Progress Update — Planned vs. Actual</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Q3 Check-in</strong></p></td><td><p>January</p></td><td><p>Progress Update — Planned vs. Actual</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Q4 / Annual</strong></p></td><td><p>March / April</p></td><td><p>Final Achievement Capture</p></td></tr></table><h1>3.  User Roles &amp; Personas</h1><p>The portal must support three distinct user roles, each with clearly differentiated access and capabilities:</p><table><tr><td><p><strong>Role</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Responsibilities</strong></p></td><td><p><strong>Required System Capabilities</strong></p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Employee</strong></p></td><td><p>Draft goals; enter quarterly achievement; update progress status</p></td><td><p>Create &amp; edit goals pre-submission; view locked goals; input actuals</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Manager (L1)</strong></p></td><td><p>Review &amp; approve goals; conduct quarterly check-ins; log feedback</p></td><td><p>Team dashboard; inline editing during approval; comment / feedback logs</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Admin / HR</strong></p></td><td><p>Configure cycles; manage org hierarchy; oversee completion rates</p></td><td><p>Cycle management; exception handling; audit logs; goal unlock capability</p></td></tr></table><h1>4.  Reporting &amp; Governance Requirements</h1><ul><li>Achievement Report: Exportable (CSV / Excel) showing Planned Target vs. Actual Achievement for all employees</li><li>Completion Dashboard: Real-time view of which employees and managers have completed quarterly check-ins</li><li>Audit Trail: System must log all changes made to goals after the lock date — capturing who changed what and when</li></ul><h1>5.  Good-to-Have Features (Bonus Points)</h1><p>While not mandatory, the following enhancements will be positively evaluated and can differentiate your solution. Teams that implement any of these will earn additional credit under the evaluation criteria.</p><h2>5.1  Microsoft Entra ID (Azure AD) Integration</h2><ul><li>Single Sign-On (SSO) for employees and managers via Microsoft Entra ID</li><li>Automatic org hierarchy sync — reporting lines derived from Azure AD attributes</li><li>Role assignment mapped from Azure AD group membership</li></ul><h2>5.2  Email &amp; Microsoft Teams Integration</h2><ul><li>Automated email notifications for key events: goal submission, approval, rejection, check-in reminders</li><li>Teams bot or adaptive card notifications for managers when a team member submits or updates goals</li><li>Deep-link support so users can navigate directly from a Teams notification to the relevant goal sheet</li></ul><h2>5.3  Escalation Module (Rule-Based)</h2><ul><li>Configurable escalation rules triggered by defined conditions, for example:</li><li>Employee has not submitted goals within N days of cycle open</li><li>Manager has not approved goals within N days of submission</li><li>Quarterly check-in not completed within the active window</li><li>Escalation chain: auto-notification to employee → manager → skip-level / HR after defined intervals</li><li>Escalation log visible to Admin / HR for tracking and resolution</li></ul><h2>5.4  Analytics Module</h2><ul><li>Quarter-on-Quarter (QoQ) goal achievement trends at individual, team, and department levels</li><li>Heatmaps or progress charts showing completion rates across the organization</li><li>Goal distribution analysis — breakdown by Thrust Area, UoM type, and status</li><li>Manager effectiveness dashboard — comparison of check-in completion rates across L1 managers</li></ul><h1>6. Constraints &amp; Ground Rules</h1><ul><li>Participants may use any technology stack of their choice (web frameworks, databases, cloud services)</li><li>The portal must be accessible via a web browser — no desktop-only applications</li><li><strong>A working demo with at least one complete user journey per role (Employee, Manager, Admin) must be presented</strong></li><li>Code must be version-controlled and the repository link submitted before the deadline</li><li>Participants must provide a brief architecture diagram explaining their technology and hosting choices</li></ul><h1>8.
